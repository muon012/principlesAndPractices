Review

1) What do we mean by "Programming is understanding"?

It means that if we understand a problem, then we can write a solution to it with code. And vice-versa.

3) How do you break a problem up into smaller manageable parts?

Look for parts of the solution that can be separately described and potentially used several times throughout the program.

4) Why is creating a small limited version of the program a good idea?

Because we often think we know how to solve the problem but a small version of the program brings out problems in our understanding, and to see if the course of action undergone to solve the problem is the appropriate one.

5) Why is a feautre creep a bad idea?

Because once we strart thinking about adding more features to the program, we keep increasing the amount of work needed to have a finished, well-tested, product.

6) What are the three stages of development?

Analysis, design, and implementation.

7) What is a use case?

It is an exmaple of an use of the program. These uses happen during analyis and design and help deciding how to proceed with the program.

12) What is a "token"?

It is a sequence of characters that represents something we consider a unit, such as a number or an operator.

13) What is a grammar? A grammar rule?

It is a set of rules that defines a syntax. A grammar rule is simply just one of those rules.

14) What is a class? What do we use classes for?

It is a user-defined type. They are useful when we want to create a type that serves a specific purpose like a point or date. They have their own data and their own set of operations.

15) How can we provide a default value for a member of a class?

We simply assign a value to it inside the class.

21) Why do we sometimes split a class' members into private and public members?

Because it separates what the user needs from the details of implementation.

26) What is the interface to a C++ "used-defined type"?

The "public" part of a class.

27) Why do we want to rely on libraries of code?

Because they have been tested already and allows to focus on other problems of our program.

Terms

    1. Parser
      A program that reads a stream of tokens according to a grammar. It is also called a "syntax analyzer".
    2. Pseudo code
      It is a way of organizing our ideas into a very general coding structure, but remembles nothing like real code.
